# Common UX Observations Across Amazon, Google Pay, and LinkedIn

## Overview
This document summarizes common UX patterns, strengths, and problems observed across three widely used applications: Amazon, Google Pay, and LinkedIn, from a student and user perspective.

## Common UX Strength
- All three applications focus on clean and structured design.
- Primary actions are visible and accessible.
- Each app serves a clear purpose for its users.

## User Experience Observations
- Amazon works well for gadget and product shopping but feels crowded for first-time users.
- Google Pay provides a fast and smooth experience for small and frequent payments.
- LinkedIn is powerful for networking but presents too much mixed content at once.

## Common UX Problems
- Interface can feel confusing for first-time users.
- Too much content on the screen leads to distraction.
- Important actions are sometimes lost among ads and suggestions.

## Suggestions for Improvement
- Provide a short guided walkthrough video for new users.
- Simplify home screen layout to reduce cognitive overload.
- Improve content filtering based on user intent.
- Strengthen security visibility in payment apps to increase user trust.

## Key Learning
Good UX should guide users clearly, reduce distraction, and help them achieve their goal with minimum effort.
